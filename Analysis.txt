Sentiment Analysis - Error Analysis

This project uses TextBlob to classify sentences as Positive, 
Negative, or Neutral based on polarity scores. While the model 
performs well on clear sentiment, it struggles with more nuanced 
language.

1. Slang / Context Example
Sentence: "This movie was sick."  
Predicted: Negative  
Actual: Positive  

Explanation: The model has difficulty interpreting slang and contextual
meaning. Although "sick" can have a positive meaning in informal language,
the model interprets it using its traditional negative meaning. This shows
that the model relies on standard word sentiment rather than understanding
modern or contextual usage.

2. Sarcasm Example
Sentence: "Great, another delay. Just what I needed."  
Predicted: Positive  
Actual: Negative  

Explanation: The model incorrectly classifies this sentence as positive
due to the presence of words like "Great." However, the sentence is
sarcastic and expresses frustration. The model cannot detect sarcasm or
implied tone, highlighting a limitation of rule-based sentiment analysis
methods.

Overall, these errors demonstrate that simple sentiment analysis models
struggle with context, tone, and linguistic complexity, especially when
meaning depends on more than individual word polarity.
